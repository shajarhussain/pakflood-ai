# Research Basis

## Pakistan Flood Science

Floods in Pakistan are a **compound hazard** driven by:

| Driver | System Feature |
|---|---|
| Extreme monsoon rainfall | Rainfall accumulation, anomaly, percentile, forecast rainfall |
| Riverine flooding (Indus Basin) | Distance to river, GloFAS discharge, FFD river state |
| Glacial melt and GLOF | Future GLOF module (northern Pakistan) |
| Antecedent wetness | 3d/7d/15d/30d rainfall features |
| Urban drainage failure | Imperviousness, roads, drains, crowd reports |
| Exposure and vulnerability | WorldPop, OSM/HDX infrastructure and agriculture layers |

## 2022 Flood Context

The 2022 Pakistan floods caused USD 14.9B in damages (World Bank/PDNA). They were linked to extreme monsoon rainfall anomalies primarily affecting Sindh and Balochistan, with hydrometeorological complexity including antecedent wetness and glacier-related hazards.

## Research Gaps This Project Addresses

1. Dashboards show maps but rarely explain **why** a location is at risk
2. Academic models predict susceptibility but lack usable HCI dashboards
3. No single interface combines rainfall anomaly, satellite extent, population exposure, and reports
4. Multi-hazard extensible architecture is rare in student/prototype projects
5. AI suggestions often appear unsupported — PakFlood AI requires sources, confidence, and disclaimers
